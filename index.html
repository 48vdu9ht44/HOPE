The Problem

Why HOPE must exist 💔

Tamil Nadu saw a 56% spike in child abuse cases in 2024. Girls in tribal villages and hill regions are the most vulnerable — and the most ignored.

📚
Zero body safety education
In rural India, safety education is considered taboo. Children grow up never learning what a safe or unsafe touch means.
80%
never tell anyone
👨‍👩‍👧
Abusers are known people
Teachers, family members, neighbours — the child's trust is used against them. Only 7% of abuse involves strangers.
93%
are known to the child
🏔️
Alone in fields and hills
Tribal girls work alone in forests and fields — no phone signal, no adult nearby, no way to call for help.
53.7%
tribal girls report abuse
🤐
Shame keeps children silent
Deep cultural stigma means even parents don't report. Only 3% of all child abuse cases are ever reported to police.
3%
cases ever reach police

Safe zone

How It Works

The reflex in action ⚡

When a private zone is touched on the HOPE doll, a chain of automatic responses fires in under a second.

1
👆
Touch detected on private zone
The MPR121 capacitive sensor pad in the doll's fabric detects a touch on the private area instantly.
Sensor fires
2
⚡
Arduino brain responds instantly
Zero delay — the Arduino Nano identifies this as a private zone and triggers all responses simultaneously.
0.1 seconds
3
💪
Doll's arm swings out — physically!
The SG90 servo motor rotates 90 degrees in 0.1 seconds. The doll's arm physically sweeps outward, pushing the hand away.
Physical reflex
4
🔊
Voice shouts "NO NO! Don't touch!"
DFPlayer plays a pre-recorded voice at maximum volume in Tamil, Hindi, or English. Red LED strobes three times.
Audio + visual alert
5
📱
HOPE app opens help screen
Bluetooth alert sends zone ID to the app — which immediately opens the Help screen with CHILDLINE 1098.
Help shown instantly
6
🔄
Arm resets. Lesson learned.
After 3 seconds, the servo returns to resting position. The child learns clearly, memorably, and safely.
System resets

HOPE delivers safety education to children in tribal and rural government schools where absolutely no such curriculum currently exists.
